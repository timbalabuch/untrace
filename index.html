    Why remove photo metadata?

    Every photo can carry hidden data — the exact GPS coordinates where it was taken, your device model, timestamps and editing history. Share the file as-is and you hand all of that to anyone who downloads it. Untrace removes it so the image you post reveals nothing but the image.

    How it works

    Drop a JPEG or PNG and Untrace strips the metadata blocks while leaving the pixels byte-for-byte identical, then lets you download the cleaned file (or a batch as a ZIP). All processing happens locally on your device.

    Frequently asked questions

    What metadata does Untrace remove?

    EXIF, GPS coordinates, camera and device info, timestamps, XMP, IPTC, C2PA and other hidden markers.

    Does it reduce image quality?

    No. It removes only the metadata blocks; the pixels stay byte-for-byte identical, so the image is unchanged.

    Are my photos uploaded anywhere?

    No. Everything runs locally in your browser — no image ever leaves your device.

    Which formats are supported?

    JPEG and PNG.

    Why remove metadata before sharing a photo?

    Photos can reveal your GPS location, device model and the exact time a shot was taken; stripping that protects your privacy.

    100% client-side — no image, byte, or pixel ever leaves your machine.
